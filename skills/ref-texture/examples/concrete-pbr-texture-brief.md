## 2. Meso Reference — Mid-Level Structure

MID-LEVEL PATTERNS:
  Pattern type:   Crack network (primary), aggregate distribution (secondary),
                  form-release banding (tertiary)
  Pattern scale:
    - Major cracks: 2–8mm wide, 0.3–1.2m long
    - Aggregate: 10–30mm diameter stones, random distribution
    - Form banding: subtle horizontal lines every 0.5–1.5m (pour sequence)
  Variation:       Highly irregular — avoid any regularity in crack placement

CONSTRUCTION DETAIL:
  Poured concrete construction logic:
  - Concrete is poured in layers (lifts). Slight color/texture variation
    between lifts is realistic and breaks monotony.
  - Aggregate is heavier than cement paste — sinks toward bottom of pour.
    Surface aggregate is less dense than aggregate visible at broken edges.
  - Formwork (the mold) leaves texture on the surface: wood grain impression
    from plywood forms is common on urban structural concrete.
  - Rebar is inside the slab. When concrete cracks and moisture enters,
    rust blooms from rebar locations and follows the structural grid.

CONSTRUCTION DETAIL QUERIES:
  - "concrete pour layer lift line texture reference photography"

## 3. Micro Reference — Surface Detail

```
SURFACE TEXTURE TYPE:
  Primary: Rough and granular (cement paste surface with fine aggregate exposure)
  Secondary: Smooth zones at high-wear contact points (ground, corners, edges)
  Hybrid: Biological growth zones have different micro-texture (porous/granular)

TEXTURE CHARACTERISTIC:
  Primary texture: Fine aggregate (sand-grain) pattern in cement paste matrix.
                   Individual grains 0.5–3mm, tightly packed, slightly irregular.
  Secondary texture: Larger aggregate exposure (10–30mm) at spalled zones,
                     micro-crack network within aggregate particles.

MICRO-VARIATION:
  Yes — significant variation:
  - Dense, smooth zones near edges (smoothed by contact and erosion)
  - Rough, porous zones at biological growth areas
  - Very rough, exposed aggregate at spalled/damaged zones
  - Fine hairline crack network throughout (even "clean" concrete has micro-cracks)

IMPERFECTION LOGIC — CONCRETE SPECIFIC:
  1. CRACKS follow structural logic:
     - Tension cracks: perpendicular to load direction (vertical in walls)
     - Compression cracks: diagonal at 45° at corners and openings
     - Settlement cracks: irregular, often following weak points in aggregate
     - Surface cracks (crazing): very fine random network, shallow
     DO NOT: place cracks randomly. Every crack has a cause.

  2. WATER STAINING is strictly gravity-driven:
     - Runs vertically downward from every horizontal surface and crack
     - Gets lighter as it moves further from source (mineral concentration drops)
     - Leaves lighter mineral deposit at evaporation line (tide mark)
     DO NOT: add horizontal water staining unless there is a horizontal source.

  3. BIOLOGICAL GROWTH follows light and moisture:
     - Moss: north-facing, sheltered, consistently moist zones
     - Lichen: can tolerate more sun than moss, grows on dry-ish surfaces
     - Algae: standing water zones, drainage paths, horizontal surfaces
     DO NOT: add biological growth on sun-facing, dry, sheltered surfaces.

  4. SPALLING (aggregate exposure) follows impact and freeze-thaw:
     - Ground level: impact from vehicles, foot traffic, thrown debris
     - Upper levels: freeze-thaw cycling, rebar expansion
     - Always exposes aggregate — shows interior texture different from surface

MICRO REFERENCE QUERIES:
  1. "concrete surface macro photography grain texture close-up"
  2. "concrete crack network macro photography fine detail"
  3. "concrete spalling aggregate exposed close-up photography"
  4. "moss concrete wall close-up micro texture"

                  This is the single best source for micro-detail reference.

## 5. Tiling and Repetition Reference

```
TILE SIZE:
  Coverage: 1m × 1m real-world (standard for hero environment props)
  Resolution: 4K (512px/m texel density at 1m tile)

TILEABILITY:
  Concrete tiles with breaks — crack network must not repeat visibly.
  The crack pattern is the element most likely to create obvious tiling.

REPETITION AVOIDANCE STRATEGY:
  1. Primary: Stochastic tiling in UE5 material (Stochastic Texture Sampling node)
     - Randomizes UV space so identical tiles never align
     - Free in UE5 material editor, document the setup for buyers

  2. Secondary: Macro variation overlay
     - Second texture at 4× scale (4m coverage) driving large-scale
       color and roughness variation (dark patches, light patches, wet zones)
     - This is the most effective single technique for hiding tiling
     - Include this as a separate texture in the pack — high value add for buyers

  3. Tertiary: Crack direction variation
     - Randomize crack direction ±30° from vertical in Substance Designer graph
     - Prevents the "same crack going the same direction everywhere" tell
