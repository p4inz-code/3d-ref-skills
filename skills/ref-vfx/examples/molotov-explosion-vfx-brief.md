```
PHASE 1 — Impact and Ignition (0–12 frames at 30fps = 0.4 seconds):
  Duration:     12 frames
  Motion type:  Rapid radial expansion, glass fragmentation, liquid scatter
  Key behavior: Burning liquid scatters outward in irregular arcs before
                main combustion ignites. The fire does NOT appear instantly —
                there is a 2–4 frame delay between impact and ignition.
  Search query: "Molotov cocktail impact slow motion footage"
  Best source:  The Slow Mo Guys YouTube — search "fire slow motion"

PHASE 2 — Primary Fireball (12–36 frames = 1.2 seconds):
  Duration:     24 frames
  Motion type:  Rapid upward expansion, asymmetric mushroom shape, turbulent edges
  Key behavior: Hot gas rises faster than the combustion spreads outward.
                Fireball is taller than it is wide within 0.5 seconds.
                Black smoke trails immediately above orange combustion zone.
  Search query: "petrol fire explosion slow motion fireball shape"
  Best source:  Military demolition footage (YouTube / public domain)

PHASE 3 — Burning Pool (36–150 frames = 4 seconds, then loops):
  Duration:     Variable — loop at approximately 30-frame cycle
  Motion type:  Low flickering flames, irregular edges, slow outward creep
  Key behavior: Pool fire is much lower energy than the fireball. Flames are
                0.3–0.5m tall, not 1.5m+. Common mistake: keeping the fireball
                scale for the pool phase.
  Search query: "petrol pool fire burning ground reference slow motion"
  Best source:  Fire safety training footage (often public domain)

PHASE 4 — Smoke Trail and Dissipation (throughout, peaks after phase 2):
  Duration:     Continuous — smoke appears from frame 1, peaks at frame 36,
                dissipates over 60–90 additional frames
  Motion type:  Rising column, turbulent folding at edges, horizontal spread
                at ceiling height (or into air)
  Key behavior: Smoke from burning petrol is very dark (near-black) —
                significantly darker than wood smoke or cigarette smoke.
                This is caused by incomplete combustion of hydrocarbons.
  Search query: "petrol fire black smoke column photography reference"
  Best source:  Fire photography, news photography (WikiMedia Commons)
```

## 3. Scale Reference

```
REAL-WORLD SIZE:
  Molotov fireball:   1.2–1.8m diameter in real world (wide variation by fuel quantity)
  Pool radius:        0.5–1.0m radius for typical 500ml bottle
  Smoke column:       Reaches 3–5m height before dissipating in still air

SCALE ANCHOR:
  Dead Corps player character = approximately 64px at standard game zoom
  Fireball at peak should be approximately 80–100px wide (slightly larger than character)
  Pool fire radius = approximately 50–60px
  If fireball is smaller than character width: too small, increase spawn rate/scale
  If fireball obscures entire screen at normal zoom: too large, reduce

COMMON SCALE MISTAKE:
  Game Molotov explosions are almost always too small and too instantaneous.
  Real petrol fire expands over 0.4 seconds before peak — it is NOT instant.
  Add a 4-frame delay before the fireball emitter activates for physical accuracy.
```

## 4. Timing Reference

```
TOTAL DURATION:     ~180 frames at 30fps = 6 seconds (explosion + 4s pool)

TIMING BREAKDOWN:
  Phase 1 (impact/ignition):  Frames 0–12    (6.7% of total)
  Phase 2 (fireball peak):    Frames 12–36   (13.3%)
  Phase 3 (pool loop):        Frames 36–150  (63.3%)
  Phase 4 (smoke decay):      Frames 36–180  (overlaps phases 3–4)

TIMING NOTES:
  The single most common timing mistake in game fire VFX:
  The explosion onset is too fast and the dissipation is too fast.
  Real petrol fire onset: 0.3–0.4 seconds. Real dissipation: 2–5+ seconds.
  Most game implementations do onset in 0.1s and dissipation in 0.5s.
  This reads as fake — the brain recognizes the incorrect timing before
  it consciously identifies why.

LOOP POINT:
  Pool fire loops at ~30 frames. Stagger pool fire instances across the radius
  by ±5 frames to prevent all flames from peaking and dipping simultaneously.
```
