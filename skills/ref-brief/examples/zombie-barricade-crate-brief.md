## 5. Detail Focus Zones

```
Zone 1 — Wood Grain and Paint Cracking:
  Why it matters:  Primary surface covers 70% of the asset — if this reads wrong,
                   the whole prop fails
  Reference type:  Macro photography of weathered painted wood, paint chip patterns
  Search query:    "weathered painted wood macro texture peeling detail"
  Common mistake:  Paint cracks that are too uniform — real cracking follows grain
                   direction and concentrates at stress points (corners, holes)

Zone 2 — Rust Bloom at Metal-to-Wood Joins:
  Why it matters:  This is where the two dominant materials interact — getting the
                   transition right makes the asset feel physically believable
  Reference type:  Macro shots of galvanized steel hardware on aged wood
  Search query:    "rusted metal bracket wood stain rust bloom close-up reference"
  Common mistake:  Rust applied uniformly — real rust blooms outward from fastener
                   and runs with gravity (vertical streaks downward)

Zone 3 — Rope and Attachment Points:
  Why it matters:  Hero detail zone — players interact visually with handles most
  Reference type:  Macro of rope tied through drilled holes, fraying at edges
  Search query:    "hemp rope knot wood hole attachment macro detail"
  Common mistake:  Rope that sits on the surface — must show indentation into wood
                   from tension, with dark shadow cavity underneath
```
